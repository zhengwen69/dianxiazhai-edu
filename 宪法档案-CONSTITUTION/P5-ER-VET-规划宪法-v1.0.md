## Chapter 2: Paper Positioning and Core Argument

### 2.1 Working Title

*Technology-Assisted Self-Directed Professional Development for Cross-Disciplinary Vocational Teachers: A Mixed-Methods Study from Chinese UAV Education*

Alternative (shorter):
*Self-Directed Technology-Assisted Professional Learning for Cross-Disciplinary VET Teachers in China*

### 2.2 Core Argument (Thesis Statement)

The NFSC (Non-Fiction Self-Study Course) + Agent methodology, originally developed and refined through five years of teaching practice in Chinese UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) vocational education, enables cross-disciplinary vocational teachers to achieve measurable teaching competence through self-directed, zero-cost technology assistance. This claim is empirically validated through multi-source, multi-stakeholder data: 10 semesters of teaching archives (2021–2025), peer teacher evaluations (N ≥ 40 from 30+ institutions), and student usability-questionnaire data (N ≥ 60).

### 2.3 Research Questions

**RQ1**: How does the NFSC+Agent self-study model support cross-disciplinary vocational teacher development, as evidenced by longitudinal teaching archives and learner outcomes?

**RQ2**: What conditions enable the transfer of this self-directed model to other vocational institutions and teacher populations, as perceived by external peer teachers?

### 2.6 Contribution Statement (for Cover Letter)

This study makes three contributions:
1. Provides the **first multi-institutional empirical validation** of a self-directed, technology-assisted professional development model (NFSC+Agent) for cross-disciplinary vocational teachers in China.
2. Introduces a **mixed-methods evaluation framework** combining teaching archives, peer review, and student usability metrics for VET teacher development.
3. Identifies **transfer conditions** under which self-directed professional learning models can be adopted by other vocational institutions.

## Chapter 3: Methodology Constitution

### 3.1 Research Design

**Mixed-Methods Design**: Convergent parallel design (QUAN + QUAL) with integration at the interpretation stage.

- **Quantitative strand**: Structured questionnaires (Likert-scale instruments) for peer teachers and students.
- **Qualitative strand**: Open-ended questionnaire responses + longitudinal teaching archive analysis.
- **Integration**: Triangulation of findings from all three data modules.

### 3.2 Participants and Sampling

| Module | Sample | Estimated N | Sampling Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Module A: Administrative | Institutional leaders/administrators from VET colleges | N ≥ 20 | Purposive (Xie Zeyu's institutional network) |
| Module B: Peer Teachers | Vocational teachers from 30+ UAV-related institutions | N ≥ 40 | Purposive + snowball through WeChat |
| Module C: Students | Students who used CD-UAV portal | N ≥ 60 | Convenience sampling via Wenjuanxing |

**Inclusion criteria for Module B**: Must view CD-UAV portal and NFSC course materials before evaluation.

### 3.3 Instruments

#### 3.3.1 Module B — Peer Teacher Evaluation Instrument

Custom 13-item Likert-scale questionnaire:
- **Items 1–5**: Perceived pedagogical effectiveness of NFSC+Agent model (5-point Likert: 1 = Strongly Disagree to 5 = Strongly Agree)
- **Items 6–9**: Perceived transferability to own institution (5-point Likert)
- **Items 10–13**: Perceived conditions/barriers for adoption (5-point Likert)
- **Additional**: 2 open-ended questions on strengths and improvement suggestions

#### 3.3.2 Module C — Student Usability Instrument

Standardized instruments:
- **System Usability Scale (SUS)** (Brooke, 1996): 10 items, 5-point Likert
- **Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)** items: Perceived Usefulness (4 items) and Perceived Ease of Use (4 items)
- **Agent Satisfaction items** (custom, 3 items): Satisfaction with AI-assisted self-study guidance
- **Additional**: 1 open-ended question on learning experience

#### 3.3.3 Module A — Administrative Context Instrument

- Institutional type, size, UAV programme status
- Current cross-disciplinary teacher ratio
- Existing professional development mechanisms
- Open-ended: Perceived challenges in cross-disciplinary teacher development

### 3.4 Data Sources

1. **Teaching Archives (10 semesters, 2021–2025)**: Course materials, student work samples, assessment records, iterative course revisions — used for qualitative contextualization and longitudinal evidence.
2. **Peer Teacher Evaluations (Module B)**: Primary data for RQ2 (transfer conditions).
3. **Student Questionnaires (Module C)**: Primary data for RQ1 (model effectiveness).

### 3.5 Data Analysis Plan

| Data Type | Analysis Method |
|---|---|
| Likert-scale data (Module B, Module C) | Descriptive statistics: mean ± SD; frequency distributions |
| Subgroup comparisons | Independent samples t-test / Mann-Whitney U: teachers with vs. without UAV background |
| SUS scores | Composite SUS score calculation (Brooke 1996 formula: score = (sum of item contributions) × 2.5, range 0–100) |
| TAM scores | Mean scores for Perceived Usefulness and Perceived Ease of Use subscales |
| Open-ended responses | Thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006): coding → categories → themes |
| Teaching archives | Document analysis: chronological mapping of pedagogical adaptations |
| Internal consistency | Cronbach's alpha for all Likert instruments |

### 3.6 Timeline

| Phase | Activity | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Module A + B data collection (Xie Zeyu's Sichuan UAV college visits) | 1–2 weeks |
| Phase 2 | Module C Wenjuanxing distribution | Concurrent with Phase 1 |

- Anonymization of all personal and institutional identifiers

- Institutional ethics clearance obtained (if required by institution)
- No vulnerable populations; minimal risk study

## Chapter 4: Empirical Data Collection Constitution

### 4.1 Three-Module Data Architecture

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                 P5 Empirical Framework               │
├───────────────┬──────────────────┬──────────────────┤
│   Module A    │    Module B      │    Module C      │
│ Admin Context │  Peer Teachers   │    Students      │
│   (N ≥ 20)    │    (N ≥ 40)      │    (N ≥ 60)      │
├───────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ Institutional │ Perceived        │ SUS + TAM +      │
│ background &  │ effectiveness &  │ Agent            │
│ PD mechanisms │ transferability  │ satisfaction     │
├───────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ Purpose:      │ Purpose: Answer  │ Purpose: Answer  │
│ Context for   │ RQ2 (transfer    │ RQ1 (model       │
│ interpretation│ conditions)      │ effectiveness)   │
└───────────────┴──────────────────┴──────────────────┘
```

### 4.2 Module A: Administrative Background

- **Target**: Institutional leaders, department heads, programme coordinators
- **N**: ≥ 20 respondents from 30+ institutions
- **Instrument**: Structured questionnaire (institutional profile + PD challenges)
- **Distribution**: Face-to-face during Xie Zeyu's college visits + WeChat follow-up
- **Purpose**: Establishes the institutional landscape of cross-disciplinary teacher development in Chinese UAV vocational education; provides context for interpreting Module B and Module C findings.

### 4.3 Module B: Peer Teacher Evaluation

- **Target**: Vocational teachers (any discipline) from 30+ UAV-related institutions
- **N**: ≥ 40 respondents
- **Prerequisite**: Must view CD-UAV portal and NFSC course materials before completing evaluation
- **Instrument**: 13-item Likert + 2 open-ended questions
- **Distribution**: Face-to-face + WeChat (questionnaire link shared after portal demonstration)
- **Purpose**: Captures external peer perception of the NFSC+Agent model's pedagogical effectiveness and transfer conditions (RQ2).

### 4.4 Module C: Student Usability Evaluation

- **Target**: Students who have used the CD-UAV learning portal
- **N**: ≥ 60 respondents
- **Instrument**: SUS (10 items) + TAM (8 items) + Agent Satisfaction (3 items) + 1 open-ended
- **Distribution**: Wenjuanxing (问卷星) online survey platform
- **Purpose**: Provides learner-side empirical evidence of model effectiveness (RQ1).

### 4.5 Data Quality Assurance

- Pre-test all instruments with 3–5 pilot respondents before full deployment
- SUS: use verified Chinese translation (if available) or back-translate from Brooke (1996)
- Ensure Wenjuanxing survey settings: one response per device, required fields
- Face-to-face Module A+B: researcher present to clarify items but not influence responses
- Minimum N targets are floors, not ceilings — aim higher where feasible

### 4.6 Data Storage and Management

- Raw data stored in password-protected Excel/CSV files
- Backup on institutional cloud storage and local encrypted drive
- Data retention: 5 years post-publication (Springer Nature policy)
- Data availability statement: "The datasets generated and analyzed during the current study are available from the corresponding author on reasonable request."
